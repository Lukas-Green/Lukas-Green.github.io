**The backend idea, in plain terms:**

*Staff side (private login):* A dashboard showing every active driver, the jobs they've run, the money they've made, and live job status. Filterable by driver, date, route, customer. The kind of view that's painful to pull together when the data lives across CXT, spreadsheets, and paper.

*Driver side (their own login):* A simple "what's my check going to be" view. Job history, current period earnings, paid vs unpaid, any deductions or bonuses. Mobile-first, no clutter. Drivers only see their own data, not each other's.

*CXT angle:* I'm not trying to rip out something that works. The thinking is, if there are pieces of CXT that your team doesn't really use, or that feel clunky, those are the pieces worth replacing first. The rest can stay until it makes sense to consolidate. Could end up saving on per-driver licensing depending on what your setup looks like.

**A few questions to figure out if this is worth pursuing:**

1. How many drivers are active right now, and how do you currently handle pay calculation (CXT export, manual, hybrid)?
2. What does CXT do well for you, and what frustrates you about it?
3. Who on the staff side needs visibility into driver activity and money (you, Sue, dispatch, accounting)?
4. Roughly how many jobs a day move through the system?
5. If a driver wanted to check what they were making mid-week, can they today, or do they have to ask?
6. Is there anything you wish you could see at a glance that you can't right now?

If any of this lands, I can walk you through the mockup live and we can scope what actually makes sense for Courier Direct. No pressure either way. Just thought it was worth showing you.

# PLAN A: DIRECT SELLING MACHINE

**Target:** $50K+/month in gross sales within 6 months. Premier Shop status by month 4.

---

## Phase 1: Foundation (Week 1-2)

### Business Setup
- Form an LLC in your state ($50-$150 filing fee). Single-member LLC for simplicity.
- Open a business bank account (Mercury or Relay — no fees, instant setup).
- Get a resale certificate in your home state — lets you buy inventory tax-free from wholesalers.
- Register for sales tax automation via TaxJar ($19/mo) or Avalara — marketplaces like Whatnot collect/remit for you, but you need this for off-platform sales.

### Platform Setup
- Apply to sell on Whatnot (approval typically within days).
- Complete your profile: professional photo, bio emphasizing your niche, social links.
- Apply to the Whatnot Affiliate Program simultaneously at whatnotaffiliates.com.

### Niche Selection (Pick ONE to start)

| Niche | Why | Startup Capital | Margin |
|-------|-----|-----------------|--------|
| **Sports Cards** | 13,500+ live streams active. WNBA cards +1,670% YoY. Huge community. | $1K-$5K | 50-200%+ on graded |
| **Women's Fashion** | ~13,000 streams. Plus-size is underserved with fanatical repeat buyers. | $500-$2K | 5-20x on thrifted |
| **Vintage Clothing** | Buy at $2-10/piece, sell at $20-100+. High entertainment value on stream. | $300-$1K | 5-50x |
| **Coins & Money** | Only 4% commission (vs 8%). Passionate collector base. | $2K-$10K | 30-100% |
| **Electronics** | Only 5% commission. High AOV. | $2K-$5K | 20-40% |

**Decision framework:** Pick the niche where you have existing knowledge OR the highest margin-to-capital ratio for your budget.

### Equipment (Day 1 Kit — $300-$500)
- Phone (iPhone 12+ or recent Android) as primary camera
- Ring light ($30)
- Clip-on wireless lavalier mic ($40)
- Small product display stand/backdrop ($20)
- Shipping supplies: poly mailers, boxes, tape, thermal label printer ($150)
- **Total: ~$270-$450**

## Phase 2: Launch & Learn (Week 3-6)

### Inventory Sourcing (First Batch: $500-$2,000)

**Tier 1 — Immediate (Day 1):**

- Local thrift stores: Goodwill, Salvation Army, Value Village

**Tier 2 — Week 2+:**
- Liquidation pallets: 888 Lots, DirectLiquidation, B-Stock, BULQ

**Tier 3 — Month 2+:**
- Wholesale accounts with distributors in your niche
- Consignment from local collectors/retailers (see Plan E)
- Storage unit auctions via StorageTreasures.com

### First Shows
- **Schedule 3 shows in week 1.** Stream Thu/Fri/Sat at 7-9 PM your timezone.
- First shows: 20-40 items each. Mix auctions ($1 start) and Buy-It-Now.
- **$1 start auctions create urgency and draw crowds.** Use them for your first 5-10 items per show to build viewership.
- Run 1-2 giveaways per show (low-value items) to grow followers.
- Keep shows 60-90 minutes max until you build stamina.

### Show Structure (Proven Format)
```
0:00-0:05   Welcome, preview tonight's items, hype the audience
0:05-0:20   $1 start auctions (loss leaders / crowd builders)
0:20-0:50   Main inventory (mix auction + BIN at healthy margins)
0:50-1:00   "Fire sale" — remaining items at deep discounts
1:00-1:10   Giveaway, thank viewers, announce next show
```

## Phase 3: Scale (Month 2-4)

### Ramp Streaming Frequency
- **Month 2:** 4 shows/week (Tue, Thu, Fri, Sat)
- **Month 3:** 5 shows/week (Mon-Fri or Tue-Sat)
- **Sellers streaming 3-4x/week earn 40-70x more than 1-2x/month.** This is the single biggest lever.

### Hit Premier Shop Requirements
Track these metrics weekly:

| Metric | Requirement | How to Hit It |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Sales | $50K in 90 days | ~$555/day = $2.7K/show at 4 shows/week |
| Orders | 250 in 90 days | ~3 orders/show = easy at scale |
| Shows | 10 in 90 days | You'll have 40+ if streaming 4x/week |
| Ship time | 95% within 48 hrs | Batch print labels morning after show, drop at USPS same day |
| Order success | 99%+ | Never cancel. Accurately describe condition. |
| Buyer response | 95% within 48 hrs | Check messages 2x/day minimum |

**Premier Shop = 10% fee reduction (8% → 7.2%) + boosted visibility + priority support.** This compounds — more visibility = more sales = more profit.

### Production Upgrade ($500-$1,000)
- Add second camera (Sony ZV-1 II or similar) for product close-ups
- Switch to OBS with WHIP protocol (wired ethernet mandatory)
- Proper lighting: 100W LED panels ($80-$150)
- Branded overlays (Canva or Fiverr — $50-$100)
- Dedicated streaming desk/area

### Shipping Optimization
- **Thermal label printer** (DYMO or Rollo — $100-$200) eliminates tape-on labels
- **uLabels** (ulabels.app) — AI-powered label extraction for Whatnot orders
- **Pirate Ship** for cheapest USPS/UPS rates on manual shipments
- Batch all labels morning after show. One daily USPS drop-off.
- **At 100+ orders/week:** evaluate ShipStation ($25/mo) for automation

## Phase 4: Operate at Scale (Month 5-12)

### Hire (When shows exceed your capacity)

**Hire #1: Chat Moderator / Assistant** ($15-$20/hr, 3-4 hrs/show)
- Manages chat, answers buyer questions during stream
- Stages next items while you present current one
- Uses Whatnot's built-in Co-Host and Moderator roles

**Hire #2: Shipping Clerk** ($15-$18/hr, part-time)
- Batch prints labels, packs orders, handles USPS drop-offs
- Frees you to source inventory and stream

**Hire #3: Show Host** ($20-$35/hr + 2-5% commission on sales)
- Enables you to run shows you don't host
- Recruit from Whatnot seller community (they already know the platform)
- Multiple hosts = multiple simultaneous streams = multiplicative revenue

### 3PL Transition (At 500+ orders/month)
- Evaluate ShipMonk, Launch Fulfillment, or Fulfill.com
- They store inventory, pick/pack, ship — you focus on sourcing and streaming
- Cost: $3-$7 per order depending on volume and item size

### Revenue Math at Scale
```
5 shows/week × $3,000 avg gross/show = $60,000/month gross
- Whatnot fees (11.2% with Premier): -$6,720
- COGS (assume 40% of gross): -$24,000
- Shipping costs: -$3,000
- Tools/software: -$200
- Staff (mod + shipper): -$3,000
= NET PROFIT: ~$23,000/month

With 2 hosts running 10 shows/week total at $5K/show avg:
$200,000/month gross → ~$70,000-$80,000/month net
```

- This is ToS-safe: you're reading YOUR OWN seller data, not scraping the platform

**Payment infrastructure:**

## Product: "WhatnotData" (Working Name — or integrate into WhatnotPro)

### What You're Building

A searchable database of Whatnot sale prices, trends, and market intelligence that helps sellers:
1. **Price items correctly** — "What did this Pokemon card sell for on Whatnot last month?"
2. **Identify hot categories** — "What's trending up/down?"
3. **Find arbitrage** — "This sells for $X on eBay but $Y on Whatnot"
4. **Time their shows** — "This category sells best on Friday nights"

### Data Collection Strategy

**Method 1: Your Own Sales Data (ToS-Safe)**
- Your Plan A selling generates real transaction data
- Your Chrome extension (Plan B) collects opted-in user data
- Aggregate and anonymize across all WhatnotPro users
- This is the Jungle Scout model: users opt in, data gets pooled

**Method 2: Public Whatnot Data**
- Whatnot show pages, seller profiles, and listed items are publicly visible
- Collect: category, listed price, item description, show timing, seller metrics
- Use Playwright/Puppeteer on a schedule — NOT high-frequency scraping
- Store in PostgreSQL with full-text search

**Method 3: Cross-Platform Price Comparison**
- eBay completed listings API (official, free with developer account)
- Compare eBay sold prices to Whatnot sold prices in same categories
- Surface arbitrage: "Buy on eBay at $X, sells on Whatnot for $Y"

**Method 4: Official Seller API (When Available)**
- Whatnot's Seller API is in Developer Preview
- When they open applications, your WhatnotPro user traction is your ticket in
- Official API data is the holy grail — real-time, sanctioned, reliable

**Target:** $30K-$100K+/month gross within 6 months. Zero inventory risk. Scales with people, not capital.

## Why This is the Leverage Play

You sell OTHER PEOPLE'S inventory on Whatnot. They have the product. You have the platform expertise, the audience, and the production setup. You take a cut.

- Estate executors sitting on $50K+ of collectibles they can't sell
- Retail stores closing/liquidating and don't know Whatnot exists
- Collectors with thousands of items who won't go on camera
- Brands wanting to reach Whatnot's 20M+ user base but lacking live-selling expertise

## Business Model

### Tier 1: Consignment (Default)
- Client ships or drops off inventory
- You photograph, list, present on live shows, ship to buyers
- **Split: 60% to you / 40% to consignor** (standard for full-service)
- Minimum consignment value: $500 (filters out low-value time-wasters)
- Payment to consignor: NET 14 after Whatnot payout clears

### Tier 2: White-Label Shows
- You run shows under the CLIENT'S Whatnot account
- They keep the audience/followers long-term
- **Fee: 30-40% of gross sales + $500 setup fee**
- Best for brands/retailers who want their own presence

### Tier 3: Consulting & Production
- Train their staff to run Whatnot shows
- Set up their streaming equipment and OBS
- **Fee: $2,000-$5,000 per engagement + $500/hour ongoing**
- One-time revenue but leads to Tier 1/2 contracts

## Operations

### Client Acquisition

**Outbound (Week 1-4):**
- Contact local estate sale companies — pitch "we sell your leftovers on Whatnot for a cut"
- Reach out to closing retail stores (check local business journals, Google "going out of business" + your city)
- Post in collector Facebook groups: "I sell collections on Whatnot — consignment available"

- Partner with estate attorneys who handle inheritance liquidations

**Inbound (Month 2+):**
- Your YouTube content (Plan C) drives inbound leads

- Google Ads targeting "sell my [cards/coins/collectibles/vintage] collection"
- Referral bonus: $100 to anyone who sends a consignment client that closes

### Consignment Contract (Key Terms)

Every consignment client signs a contract covering:
- Commission split (60/40 or negotiated)
- Minimum consignment period (30-90 days)
- Pricing authority (you set prices; client can set minimums/reserves)
- Unsold item handling (return to client, reduce price, or donate after X days)
- Liability and insurance (client retains ownership until sold; you carry basic business insurance)
- Payment schedule (NET 14 from Whatnot payout)
- Condition disputes (items photographed on receipt; condition documented)

Use LegalZoom, Rocket Lawyer, or PandaDoc for template — customize with a local attorney ($300-$500).

- Every consigned item tagged with consignor ID, cost basis (consignor's minimum), and category
- Real-time tracking: which items are listed, in a show queue, sold, shipped
- Automated payout calculation: sale price - Whatnot fees - your commission = consignor payment

# PLAN F: AUTOMATED SOURCING & ARBITRAGE ENGINE

**Target:** Reduce sourcing time by 80%. Identify 50+ high-margin items/week automatically. Feed Plans A and E.

---

## The Problem with Manual Sourcing

Manually browsing eBay, thrift stores, estate sales, and liquidation sites burns 15-20 hours/week for serious sellers. The sellers who win at scale automate this.

## The Arbitrage Engine

### Component 1: eBay → Whatnot Price Gap Monitor

**How it works:**
- Pull eBay completed/sold listings via eBay Browse API (official, free)
- Compare to Whatnot sold prices (from your Plan D data)
- Surface items where: Whatnot avg sale price > eBay avg sale price + fees + shipping
- Alert you when margin exceeds your threshold (e.g., 40%+)

**Example:**
```
Item: 1987 Topps Baseball Complete Set (PSA graded)
eBay avg sold: $180
Whatnot avg sold: $310
Whatnot fees (~12%): -$37
Shipping: -$12
NET MARGIN: $81 (45%)
→ ALERT: Buy on eBay, sell on Whatnot
```

**Tech:** Python script, eBay API, PostgreSQL, daily cron job, email/SMS alerts via Twilio or SendGrid.

### Component 2: Liquidation Deal Scanner

**Monitor these sources automatically:**
- 888lots.com — parse new lot listings daily
- DirectLiquidation.com — API available for manifested pallets
- B-Stock (bstock.com) — retailer return pallets
- BULQ.com — curated liquidation lots
- shopgoodwill.com — online Goodwill auctions (scrape ending-soon items)

**Alert criteria:**
- Category matches your niche
- Price per unit < 30% of estimated resale value
- Lot size within your storage/handling capacity
- Condition acceptable (new/like-new/used-good)

### Component 3: Estate Sale Aggregator

- Pull from EstateSales.net API/RSS
- Filter by: location (within driving radius), categories, dates
- Auto-generate a weekly "sourcing trip" plan with optimized driving route
- Flag sales with your target categories (cards, coins, vintage, etc.)

### Component 4: Trending Category Detector

- Track Whatnot search volume and show count by category (from Plan D data)
- Identify categories with rising demand but flat/falling supply
- Alert: "Pokemon Base Set searches up 40% this week but show count flat — opportunity"
- Cross-reference with Google Trends for external demand signals

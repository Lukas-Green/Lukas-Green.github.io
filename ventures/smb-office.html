HR Time on Admin

HR is the fastest-growing segment of business process automation. Companies with mature onboarding programs are 103% more likely to improve employee retention and see a 50% reduction in time-to-productivity. Yet HR departments still spend 57% of their time on administrative tasks — payroll processing, leave management, compliance documentation — that could be automated.

The Pain

A single new hire generates 50+ documents

Every new hire triggers a cascade of paperwork: employment agreements, tax documents, benefits enrollment, equipment requests, policy acknowledgments, compliance training. Most companies manage this with a combination of email, shared drives, and hope.

  • Document Chaos: W-4, I-9, NDA, direct deposit, benefits enrollment, emergency contact, handbook acknowledgment, equipment agreement — tracked across email, shared drives, and physical folders.
  • No Visibility: Managers can't see where a new hire is in the process. HR can't tell which documents are missing until someone asks.
  • Inconsistency: Every manager onboards differently. Some hires get a thorough first week, others get a laptop and a "good luck."
  • Compliance Risk: Missing I-9 forms, late benefits enrollment, incomplete safety training — each one is a liability.
  • IT Provisioning: Account creation, software licenses, hardware setup — often the last thing done and the first thing needed.
The Solution

One workflow. Every hire. Full visibility.

OnboardFlow AI replaces the spreadsheet-and-email onboarding with a visual workflow builder that ensures every step happens, every document is collected, and every stakeholder knows their role.

  • Template-Driven Workflows: Pre-built templates for standard employees, contractors, and executives — customizable per company, per role, per department.
  • Phase-Based Steps: Organized by Pre-Start, Day One, First Week, First Month. Each step has an owner, a due date, and a status.
  • Document Tracking: Checklist per hire with received/pending status, auto-reminders, and one-click compliance summary generation.
  • Progress Visibility: Every hire has a progress bar. Managers see On Track / At Risk / Overdue at a glance.
The best onboarding process is the one that doesn't depend on any single person remembering what comes next.
Architecture

LLM onboarding adapts to your company

The AI asks: How many employees? What industry? What's your current onboarding process? What tools do you use? It generates a customized workflow template with steps specific to your industry, compliance requirements, and company culture.

  • Industry-specific templates: marketing agencies get different steps than law firms
  • Compliance-aware: auto-includes required forms and training based on jurisdiction
  • Integration-ready: connects to HRIS, payroll, and IT provisioning tools via API
  • Natural language workflow editing: describe a step and the AI adds it to the right phase
Competitive Landscape

Gusto does payroll. BambooHR does records. Neither does onboarding well.

  • Enterprise: Workday, SAP SuccessFactors — comprehensive but $50K+ and 6+ month implementation
  • Mid-Market: BambooHR, Gusto — good at their core function (records, payroll) but onboarding is an afterthought
  • Workflow: monday.com, Zapier — generic workflow tools that require custom setup for every process
  • The gap: Purpose-built onboarding with AI customization, industry templates, compliance tracking, and document management — at SMB pricing

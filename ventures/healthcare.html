Practices Using Cloud

Healthcare SaaS is one of the fastest-growing vertical segments. The Operational & Management Solutions subsegment is growing fastest at 22.58% CAGR, driven by demand for automated scheduling and workforce optimization. Yet small practices are still running on fragmented point solutions — one tool for charting, another for billing, a third for scheduling — none of which talk to each other.

The Pain

72% of providers cite staffing as their top obstacle

Small practices are closing due to burnout. Doctors spend more time on forms than patients. The administrative burden is crushing, and the tools meant to help are part of the problem.

  • EHR/EMR Fragmentation: Clinicians bypass systems requiring more than 60 seconds per task. Data migration from legacy systems is technically complex with high clinical risk.
  • Billing & Revenue Cycle: Denial rates have surged to nearly 15% industry-wide. Small practices lack dedicated compliance teams and vendor training is often insufficient.
  • Patient No-Shows: 20-30% no-show rate in outpatient clinics, representing thousands in lost revenue per month.
  • Compliance Burden: 48% of healthcare IT managers cite data breach risks as a major deterrent. Multiple state-level AI disclosure requirements are adding complexity.
  • Prior Authorization: The bottleneck that costs hours per day. AI can handle eligibility checks in milliseconds.
The Solution

One platform. Intake to billing. HIPAA-aware.

ClaimFlow AI unifies the three systems every small practice duct-tapes together: patient intake, scheduling, and billing. Instead of training staff on three separate tools, they learn one interface that handles the full patient lifecycle.

  • AI Pre-Screening: Patients submit intake forms that are automatically triaged by urgency, routing urgent cases to immediate attention.
  • Smart Scheduling: Conflict detection, provider availability, and no-show prediction built into every booking.
  • Billing Optimization: Automated code suggestions, eligibility verification, and denial prevention before claims are submitted.
  • Compliance Automation: HIPAA documentation, audit trails, and state-level regulatory tracking maintained automatically.
The gap isn't a lack of tools. It's that Epic costs $500K to implement and SimplePractice doesn't do billing. There's nothing in between.
Architecture

LLM-customized from day one

When a practice buys the ClaimFlow template, the onboarding AI asks: What's your clinic size? What specialties? What EHR are you coming from? How do you currently handle prior auth? The system generates a customized configuration — workflows, compliance checklists, scheduling rules, and billing templates — tailored to that specific practice.

  • Local AI agents handle repetitive tasks: appointment reminders, insurance eligibility checks, and document routing
  • HIPAA-compliant data architecture with encryption at rest and in transit
  • API-first design for integration with existing EHR systems where needed
  • Progressive disclosure: start with scheduling, unlock billing and compliance as the practice grows
Competitive Landscape

The gap between enterprise and budget

Enterprise players (Epic, Cerner) dominate large hospital systems but price out small practices. Budget tools (DrChrono, SimplePractice, CureMD) lack AI capabilities and comprehensive automation. The mid-market — practices with 2-15 providers — is chronically underserved.

  • Enterprise: Epic, Cerner, Athenahealth — $100K-$500K implementation, 12+ month rollout
  • Budget: SimplePractice, DrChrono, CureMD — affordable but limited automation, minimal AI
  • The gap: AI-powered scheduling + billing + compliance in one tool, at SMB pricing, with LLM-driven customization

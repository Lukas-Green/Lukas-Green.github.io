The problem we're solving

Half of everyone released from prison in Oregon will go back.

Recidivism isn't just a statistic — it's a cycle that destroys families, strains communities, and costs taxpayers billions. People leaving incarceration need more than freedom. They need skills, connection, and a reason to believe change is possible.

Better Neighbors exists because everyone deserves a chance to rebuild their lives. We provide programming and educational opportunities to marginalized populations — especially those affected by incarceration and addiction — creating safe places that provide learning and growing opportunities.

Stories of change

Transformation, told by the people living it.

Founder

"After 24 years in and out of prison, I decided I wanted to make a change."

Kirk Charlton created Art Inside Out while incarcerated at Eastern Oregon Correctional Institution. What started as a way to teach fellow inmates life lessons through art became a full program — now running at multiple facilities and community centers across Oregon.

Kirk Charlton Founder, Art Inside Out

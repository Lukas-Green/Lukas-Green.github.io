The problem we're solving

Most people don't know the neighbors three doors down.

Isolation isn't just lonely — it's a public health crisis. It erodes safety, stalls economic mobility, and leaves residents without the informal support that makes neighborhoods work.

Creating Better Neighbors exists because thriving places aren't built by programs alone. They're built by people who know each other's names. We make that first introduction radically easier — then we stay, and resource the work residents are already doing on their own blocks.

01

Relationships first

We start with trust, not forms. No program works without it.

02

Resident-led

Neighbors design the programs on their own blocks. We resource the plan.

What we do

Four programs. One model. Built by neighbors, for neighbors.

Each program is designed to remove a specific barrier to connection, then hand the keys back to residents.

The Porch Project

Monthly block parties hosted by a neighbor, funded and staffed by us. Low lift for the host, high trust for the block.

  • 312 active blocks
  • $180 avg. host grant
Host your block →

Neighbor Navigators

Trained residents help their neighbors access housing, food, childcare, and benefits — in language, in person, no bureaucracy.

  • 84 navigators
  • 6,200 assists / yr
Become a navigator →

Block Grants

Residents pitch a project — a community fridge, a repair day, a summer library — and we cut a check within 14 days. No 30-page applications.

  • 148 grants / yr
  • 14 days avg. approval
Apply for a grant →

Civic Academy

A 6-week program that turns engaged neighbors into community leaders — budget literacy, public speaking, organizing, and local policy.

  • 3 cohorts / yr
  • 96% completion
Join the next cohort →

# Kirk Charlton / Art Inside Out / Better Neighbors — Research Brief

*Compiled 2026-07-14 via deep-research harness (103 agents, 20 sources, 25 claims adversarially verified). For the AIO + Better Neighbors redesign. Oregon Harbor of Hope = context only.*

---

## 1. Kirk Charlton — VERIFIED bio (safe to publish)

- **~20 years cumulatively incarcerated**, with an **~8-year final stretch at Eastern Oregon Correctional Institution (EOCI)** before release. The "**24 years in and out**" figure often quoted is a *different metric* — the span of the in-and-out cycle, not time behind bars. **Keep these three numbers separate; never conflate them** (~20 yrs cumulative / 8-yr EOCI stretch / 24-yr in-and-out span).
- Created the **Art Inside Out (AIO)** program *while incarcerated at EOCI*. PCC describes it as "one of the most attended programs currently offered in the Department of Corrections" — but that is **PCC's characterization from Kirk's own narrative, not a DOC statistic**. Publish only as *"PCC described it as…"*, never as a hard number.
- Solo drawing exhibition **"My Personal Experience," PCC Cascade Paragon Arts Gallery** (815 N Killingsworth, Portland), **May 4 – June 4, 2022** — 40+ mostly black-and-white pencil/pen drawings of life behind bars. Fully documented by PCC (primary) + EverOut.
- Published author: **"Art Inside Out: A Self-directed Art Program with Insights for a Positive Life"** and **"Art Inside Out: Recovery Edition."** Book co-authors: **Dr. Bud Beamer, MD** and **Rev. Jenn Dafoe-Turner** (both verified).
- Press: KATU (feature + AM Northwest), KBOO "Prison Pipeline" (Aug 2023 + Oct 2025), East Oregonian (2018 "inner artist roams free"), The Columbian.

## 2. Art Inside Out — VERIFIED program facts

- **Twelve-lesson, self-directed art program** "with insights for a positive life" (matches the book).
- **Philosophy:** *"A billion wrongs in drawing makes a right… these lines tell you where not to go."* — un-erased mistake lines show where not to go. (Direct Charlton quote, KATU.)
- **Audiences (as marketed):** incarcerated people, recovery/addiction groups, seniors/elderly, kids & youth, business groups.
- **Community classes (verified on artinsideout.net):** ADX Portland (**$60**) and Hopewell Hub, Salem (**$45**). These are the *only* dollar figures the program itself actually publishes.
- **CAVEAT:** the claim AIO is used *"extensively at OSCI plus nursing homes / mental-health facilities"* was **refuted 0-3** — that specific "extensively" phrasing is not verified. Say "has been used in" not "extensively."

## 3. THE THREE HEADLINE STATS — verdict (this is the whole point)

| Claim (currently on the sites) | Verdict | What to do |
|---|---|---|
| **5% AIO recidivism vs. 50% Oregon** | ❌ **KILLED 0-3 — UNSOURCED** | Do NOT republish as fact. Traces only to one KATU feature quoting Kirk. No study, cohort size, or DOC data. Also "50%" only matches Oregon's *arrest* rate (51.2%); official *reconviction* is 40.5%, *reincarceration* 12.5% (Jan–Jun 2018 cohort). |
| **$300 per participant / 12 weeks** | ❌ **KILLED 0-3 — UNSOURCED** | Do NOT republish as fact. No program budget behind it; only KATU quoting Kirk. If used, label as an internal estimate. |
| **~$50,000/yr to incarcerate** | ✅ **TRUE but UNDERSTATED** | Oregon DOC official = **$173.88/day ≈ $63,466/yr** (2023-2025 biennium). **Re-source to oregon.gov/doc and update to ~$63,000/yr.** Every current official figure exceeds $50k. |

**Implication:** the emotional/fundraising core of both current sites rests on two numbers that failed verification. The redesign must replace them with honest, sourced framing (see decision below).

## 4. Better Neighbors — org facts

- **Robin Davidson = Founder / Director.** **Kirk Charlton = Program Consultant / AIO Creator** (the org's own About page does **not** title him co-founder — mirror this to stay accurate, even though press calls them co-founders of the *program*).
- Operates **under / in collaboration with Oregon Harbor of Hope.** **CAVEAT:** no single primary source uses the exact legal term **"fiscal sponsorship"** — KATU frames Better Neighbors as OHOH's *internal program*; a Meetup listing frames them as *separate collaborating nonprofits*. **Verify the exact legal/tax structure with Robin before publishing "501(c)(3)" or "fiscal sponsor" as a legal descriptor.** (A ProPublica nonprofit record, EIN 320517677, surfaced but was not confirmed as Better Neighbors' own.)

## 5. Oregon Harbor of Hope (context)

- Portland homelessness-services nonprofit, 501(c)(3), founded by **Homer Williams** (real-estate developer, Pearl District / South Waterfront) after a 2016 visit to San Antonio's *Haven for Hope*, with **Don Mazziotti**. Model: **incubate initiatives into self-sustaining nonprofits.**
- Per OHOH's own written content (Optilusionary archive), it lists **both Better Neighbors AND Art Inside Out as programs it supports/incubates.** Team: Homer Williams (Chair/Founder), Matthew Bordanaro (Development Director), Patrizia Montanari (Creative Consultant), plus Robin & Kirk; Sister Maura declined a team listing.
- Hierarchy for IA: **OHOH (fiscal sponsor/incubator) → Better Neighbors (nonprofit) → Art Inside Out (flagship program).**

Our Approach
We treat the person, not the appointment slot.

The Villa was built around a simple frustration: most clinics measure success in throughput. We measure it in whether you actually feel better six months from now. That changes how we listen, how we prescribe, and how long we sit with you on a hard day.

Our care is integrative, not alternative. That means evidence-based medicine—labs, pharmacology, established protocols—applied with the time and attention it takes to actually understand what’s going on. Mental health, hormones, metabolism, and lifestyle aren’t separate departments here. They’re one conversation.

i.

Listen longer

Initial visits are 60 minutes. Follow-ups don’t end mid-sentence.

ii.

Prescribe carefully

The right medication and dose, not the easiest one. Often the answer is fewer prescriptions, not more.

iii.

Coordinate, always

Your psychiatry, hormones, and primary care talk to each other—because they’re in the same room.

iv.

Respect your time

Online intake, transparent pricing, and visits that start when they’re scheduled.

Common questions

A few things people
ask us first.

Do you accept my insurance?
We’re in-network with most major Washington and Oregon plans, including Providence, Regence, Premera, Kaiser PPO, and several Medicare Advantage plans. We’ll verify your benefits before your first visit so there are no surprises.
Are you accepting new patients?
Yes—most of our clinicians have openings within 1–3 weeks. The clinician cards above show current availability. New psychiatric intakes are typically scheduled within 7–10 days.
Do you offer telehealth?
Yes. Most of our psychiatry, hormone, and follow-up visits are available via secure video. Aesthetic, lab draws, and some intake visits are in-person at our Vancouver office.
What ages do you treat?
Most providers see patients ages 12 or 14 and up. Aesthetic services are 21+. See each clinician’s card for specifics.
How long is a first visit?
Initial visits are 60 minutes. Follow-ups are typically 30–45 minutes. We don’t double-book.
Do you prescribe controlled medications?
When clinically appropriate, yes—including stimulants for ADHD and MAT for substance use. We follow Washington state guidelines and require an in-person evaluation for new controlled prescriptions.

# Points Exchange — Market Research
**Date:** 2026-07-15
**Agent:** RADAR

## Executive Summary
2026 is the worst year on record for loyalty point devaluation velocity — Hyatt announced a 67% increase in top-tier award rates (effective May 7), Hilton has been quietly inflating dynamic pricing, and Capital One cut Emirates transfer ratios in January. This accelerating devaluation creates urgency-driven demand for a brokering/exchange product. Points.com (acquired by Plusgrade in 2022) remains the dominant institutional player; no new broker startup failures detected. Blockchain tokenized loyalty remains enterprise-facing and legally complex; not a near-term threat to the marketplace model.

## Market Trends

- **Source:** [Hyatt Award Chart Changes 2026 — NerdWallet](https://www.nerdwallet.com/travel/news/hyatt-award-chart-changes-2026)
- **Finding:** World of Hyatt announced Feb 25, 2026 a move from 3-tier to 5-tier award chart, effective May 7, 2026. Category 8 top tier: 45,000 → 75,000 points (+67%). The largest single devaluation in Hyatt's history.
- **Impact:** high
- **Action:** Devaluations drive consumer urgency to liquidate/exchange points before further erosion. Points Exchange should launch MVP during active devaluation cycles. This is the best consumer acquisition environment in years.

---

- **Source:** [Hotel and Airline Points Devaluation 2026 — Cash Balancer](https://www.cashbalancer.com/blog/hotel-airline-points-devaluation-2026)
- **Finding:** "Devaluations happening at faster cadence than at any time in modern history." Hilton dynamic pricing increases of 5,000–30,000+ points per night documented March 2026. Capital One devalued Emirates transfers from 1:1 to 1,000:750 effective January 13, 2026.
- **Impact:** high
- **Action:** Market framing for Points Exchange: "Your points are worth more today than they will be tomorrow." Devaluation urgency is a primary conversion driver.

---

- **Source:** [8 Loyalty Trends 2026 — Currency Alliance](https://www.currencyalliance.com/insights/8-loyalty-trends-for-2026-ai-hands-power-to-the-consumer)
- **Finding:** 2026 trend: AI is helping consumers optimize point usage and more brands are making "earn other brands' points" a leading value prop. Loyalty currency consolidation expected as consumers can't track dozens of currencies.
- **Impact:** medium
- **Action:** Points Exchange's aggregation/exchange model is aligned with the consolidation trend. Multi-currency wallet concept is validated by industry direction.

---

- **Source:** [Blockchain Loyalty Programs Legal & Token Risks — Aurum Law](https://aurum.law/newsroom/From-Points-to-Tokens-Blockchain-Loyalty-Programs)
- **Finding:** Blockchain loyalty token projects face securities classification risk (Howey test exposure), KYC/AML compliance burdens, and cross-border regulatory complexity. Enterprise adoption exists but legal risk is high for new entrants.
- **Impact:** low
- **Action:** Points Exchange should avoid blockchain-native architecture in MVP. Stick to traditional marketplace model. Revisit blockchain layer only after regulatory clarity emerges.

---

- **Source:** [Loyalty Points Legal & Regulatory Matters — Grom / Medium](https://medium.com/gromorg/loyalty-points-legal-regulatory-matters-681c8f50976a)

- **Impact:** medium
- **Action:** Points Exchange should review terms-of-service of target loyalty programs before launch — most major airlines prohibit point sale, creating legal risk for the broker model (similar to gift card gray market). Legal review required before MVP.

- **No loyalty point exchange regulation enacted in 2026.** The legal status of loyalty points as a currency or property right remains ambiguous in the US.

- **GDPR + ASC-606:** Applicable compliance frameworks for any loyalty data platform operating internationally.

## Signals Detected

> **GROWTH — Historic devaluation cycle creates ideal launch window**

Hyatt's 67% rate increase, Hilton dynamic inflation, and Capital One partner cuts are pushing consumers to seek alternatives. A marketplace that helps them exchange or liquidate before further erosion has a natural, urgent value proposition right now.

> **RISK — Airline ToS prohibition is the existential legal risk**
> Most major airline programs prohibit point sale in their terms of service. A consumer-facing broker that facilitates prohibited transfers faces account termination for users and potential program lawsuits. Legal strategy must be resolved before MVP launch. Options: (1) focus only on programs that allow gifting/transfer, (2) build a gray-market model with disclosed risk, (3) pivot to a B2B institutional model like Plusgrade.

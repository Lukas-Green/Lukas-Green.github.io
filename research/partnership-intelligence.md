# Partnership Intelligence — Business Development Pipeline

## 1. Executive Summary

This is a scored, searchable pipeline of 45 companies and organizations where Lukas Green's skill mix — 22+ years UX design, AI workflow automation, vertical SaaS architecture, training, consulting, and documentation — creates real leverage. Each target is evaluated on a 10-dimension rubric (50 points max) measuring alignment, viability, revenue potential, UX/design gaps, documentation needs, consulting accessibility, training opportunity, non-tech blind spots, ease of entry, and long-term upside.

**Top 5 immediate-action targets:**
1. **OpenClaw** (45/50) — Open-source AI agent with 250K+ GitHub stars and documented severe UX problems. Massive community, open contribution model, high-impact opportunity.
2. **SBA AI Training Act** (42/50) — Federal grant program launching late 2026 for organizations providing AI training to small businesses. Direct match for every service Lukas offers.
3. **0G Labs** (40/50) — $350M-funded decentralized AI OS. Fast-moving mainnet launch needs developer onboarding, visual learning content, and UX for rapidly evolving platform.
4. **Abridge** (39/50) — Healthcare AI at $5.3B valuation. 150+ health systems deployed. Clinician training on AI outputs and EHR integration documentation are critical gaps.
5. **ASI Alliance** (38/50) — Merger of Fetch.ai + SingularityNET + Ocean Protocol. Post-merger integration creates massive UX consolidation and onboarding redesign opportunity.

**Service opportunity breakdown across all 45 targets:**
- UX/Design consulting: 38 targets
- Documentation/help content: 35 targets
- Training program development: 32 targets
- Onboarding flow design: 30 targets
- Strategic consulting (non-tech): 22 targets
- Grant-funded work: 12 targets

## 2. Scoring Methodology

Each company is scored 1–5 across 10 dimensions. Total possible: 50.

| # | Dimension | 1 (Low) | 3 (Medium) | 5 (High) |
|---|-----------|---------|------------|----------|
| 1 | **Alignment** | No overlap with Lukas's work | Some shared vertical or method | Direct mission/product overlap |
| 2 | **Viability** | Pre-product, unfunded | Seed/Series A, shipping | Series B+, revenue, proven team |
| 3 | **Revenue Potential** | Unlikely to pay $175/hr | Could support project-based | Budget for ongoing retainer |
| 4 | **UX/Design Gap** | Polished, mature UX | Some rough edges | Documented severe UX problems |
| 5 | **Documentation Gap** | Comprehensive docs exist | Gaps in onboarding/training | Missing or severely lacking |
| 6 | **Consulting Entry** | Closed network, no path in | Hiring page or RFP process | Open grants, public applications |
| 7 | **Training/Onboarding** | Users self-serve fine | Could use better training | Users struggling, training critical |
| 8 | **Non-Tech Blind Spots** | Strong GTM/brand/strategy | Some gaps in positioning | Major gaps in strategy, branding, market awareness |
| 9 | **Accessibility** | Need warm intro + track record | Application process exists | Open-source, public grants, cold outreach works |
| 10 | **Long-term Upside** | One-off project | Multi-project potential | Recurring revenue, equity/token upside |

**Tier thresholds:**
- **Tier 1 (35–50):** Immediate revenue targets. Pursue now.
- **Tier 2 (25–34):** Strong pipeline. Build relationships, watch for openings.
- **Tier 3 (15–24):** Watch list. Monitor for changes in accessibility or funding.

## 7. Outreach Templates

### For DeAI / Web3 Companies (cold DM / email)
> Subject: Your docs are losing you developers
>
> I spent [X time] going through [Company]'s onboarding as a non-crypto-native builder. Here's what I found: [1-2 specific friction points]. I've been building AI workflow tools for 3 years with 22+ years of design experience. I specialize in making technical platforms usable for people who aren't already in your Discord. Happy to share a quick UX teardown — no pitch, just observations.

### For Healthcare / Dental Tech (email)
> Subject: Training gap for [Product] rollout
>
> I work with healthcare teams implementing AI tools. The #1 adoption barrier isn't the technology — it's that staff don't have clear training materials that map to their daily workflow. I build training programs, onboarding flows, and documentation for clinical AI products. Would a 15-minute call make sense to discuss [specific product/feature]?

### For Grant Programs (application framing)
> Position: "I build the layer between AI tools and the people who use them. My work reduces training time, increases adoption, and eliminates the documentation gap that kills most AI deployments in regulated industries."

### For Open-Source Projects (community-first)
> Approach: Contribute first. File a UX-focused issue with screenshots and specific recommendations. If the project responds well, offer a more comprehensive audit. Build credibility through visible contributions before pitching paid work.
